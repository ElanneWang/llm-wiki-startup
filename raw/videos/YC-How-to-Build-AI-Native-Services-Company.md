## Core Thesis

Some of the biggest companies of the next decade won't be software businesses, they'll be services companies like insurance carriers, law firms, and tax practices rebuilt from scratch with AI doing most of the work.

## Key Framework: AI-Native Services

### From SaaS to "Service-as-a-Software"
- Shift from selling software "co-pilots" to selling actual **outcomes**
- Instead of providing a tool for a lawyer to use, these companies *are* the law firm, powered by AI
- Targeting trillion-dollar industries

### Target Sectors
- Tax, Audit, Insurance, Legal Services, Healthcare (administrative/diagnostic parts), Logistics

### The "Sam Altman Test"
- Key metric for viability
- "As AI models get better, does this company get stronger, or does the model itself make the company obsolete?"

### Economic Moat
- Regulation is a positive "moat"
- Companies in highly regulated spaces (FDA, legal) have higher barriers to entry
- Protects them from simple AI wrappers

### Margin Potential
- Traditional service firms: ~30% margins
- AI-native services: **50%+ margins**
- Approaching software-level profitability while capturing much larger TAM

## Case Studies

### Panacea
- YC-backed AI-native service provider in regulatory space
- Provides FDA regulatory services for biotech and medtech companies
- Hires experienced FDA consultants + augments with AI platform
- **Outcome-Based Pricing** (per completed study) vs hourly rate
- Regulatory moat blocks competitors lacking domain expertise

### General Legal Team
- AI-native law firm backed by YC
- Founders: elite legal experience (Cooley, Fenwick) + technical leadership (Case Text)
- Uses "shift work" and AI to reduce cycle times
- Treats legal work as high-throughput operation

## Sector Risks & Red Flags

### The "Early Demand Trap"
- Signing too many pilot customers too early
- End up using humans to "paper over" product gaps
- Kills margins

### The "Buy vs. Build" Trap
- Trying to buy legacy service businesses to "add AI on top"
- Usually fails due to cultural and technical debt

### Physical Labor Constraints
- Avoid AI service plays involving heavy equipment or on-site labor
- These don't scale with software-like margins

### Pricing Pitfalls
- **Cost-Plus Pricing**: Caps upside, should be avoided
- **Undercutting**: Makes service look "cheap" in high-stakes fields

## Key Metrics

- **COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)**: Must trend down as percentage of revenue
- **Cycle Times**: Measure throughput, not just user growth
- **Variance**: Inconsistent output is the biggest killer
- **AI Operating Leverage**: Revenue grows much faster than human cost

## Key Insights

- Look for "Outcome" over "Tools"
- Focus on High-Trust/Low-Judgment industries
- Best opportunities: customers already outsource work + tasks repetitive enough for AI
- Avoid companies where headcount grows 1:1 with revenue
- A company 10% slower but 100% consistent > fast but hallucination-prone
- "Trust is the Product"
